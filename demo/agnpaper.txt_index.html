Language analysis

This program attempts to assist you in improving your paper. Language is ambiguous and subjective, a computer can not understand it. All results should be seen as suggestions; think about the highlighted sentences.
  • Do spell-checking (in your LaTeX editor, e.g. lyx)
  • Do grammar-checking (in LanguageTool)
  • Each paragraph should open informatively.
  • Tricky words, Prepositions & Wordiness
  • Wordiness & long sentences
  • Consistent use of tenses
  • Paragraph consistency
  • Check the visual appeal

This program may help you catch some classes of common mistakes, but does not replace careful reading and self-editing. Here are some steps:

Context-level

  • Is the text placed well in context (in relation to wider debate)?
  • Will readers from different contexts understand? Do they require more support?
  • Are there enough inter-text links? References, cross-links to sections, figures. Do you need to be more explicit?

Concept-level

  • what concept involved? How difficult are they? Do they rely on culture?
  • Have the concepts been presented clearly and in enough detail?
  • What is the argument? How complicated is it? How obvious is the logic?

Sentence-level

  • Spot inert sentences. Wordy, awkward connections. Those that do not advance the argument/text.
  • Delete whereever you can.
  • How lengthy are the sentences? How complex grammatically are the sentences? read most difficult sentence aloud.
  • Which sentence is most difficult to understand / easy to misinterpret?
  • Unclear grammar?
  • Add repetition only where useful
  • Consider different modes of presenting
  • How would one of my reader see this text?
  • Put best, shining ideas in the spotlight

Word/Phrasing

Tip: Try reading the text to yourself out loud! (alternatively, try a text-to-speech program)
  • word/phrase unfamiliar?
  • word/phrase ambiguous/confusing?
  • word/phrase synonyms, different meanings? distinct?

Repeat from above.

Slow reading, secretary-level work

  • Before doing this, it is best to take a break from your text for a day or two
  • Adopt the attitude: "There are bound to be some errors here and it is my job to find them now."
  • Reformat your text to double-spaced lines and read aloud.
  • Check completeness (internal references)
  • Check consistency (nomenclature, policies)
  • Check accuracy (of quotations, values, statistics, references).

Presentation

  • Adhere to style of publisher
  • Check figures
